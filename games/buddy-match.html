How to Play

Buddy Match is a simple pairing tool designed for onboarding programs. Enter the names of new hires and potential buddies, and the tool randomly creates pairs. The matching is intentionally random — the value is not in finding the perfect algorithmic match but in creating a starting point for human connection. The tool is designed with privacy in mind: only share buddy pairs with people in the group, and never post pairings publicly.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Enter new hire names and buddy names into the tool — one name per line.
  2. Set the group size (default: pairs of 2).
  3. Click 'Create Buddy Pairs' to randomly match people.
  4. Review the pairs and share them privately with the group.
  5. If any pairing needs adjustment, simply click again to regenerate.
  6. Encourage buddies to schedule a brief introduction within the first few days.

Tips for Hosts

  • Keep pairings private. Share buddy assignments directly, not in a public channel.
  • Random pairing is fine. The goal is to start a conversation, not to find a perfect match.
  • If someone is uncomfortable with their pairing, allow them to switch — no questions asked.
  • Combine Buddy Match with a simple prompt: 'Share one thing you wish you knew on day one.'

Best Use Cases

  • Buddy programs: The primary use case — pair new hires with experienced team members.
  • Cohort onboarding: Pair new hires with each other for peer support during onboarding.
  • Mentorship programs: Random matching for initial mentor-mentee introductions.
  • Cross-department introductions: Pair new hires with people from other teams for broader exposure.
