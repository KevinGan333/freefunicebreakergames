☁️ Team Word Cloud

Collect words from team members around a theme and display them as a visual word cloud. A creative way to capture group sentiment, ideas, or shared values.

Best forMeetings, Workshops, Remote Teams
Players5–100
Time5–10 min
FormatOnline
DifficultyEasy
🎮 Interactive Game — Play directly below

How to Play

The Team Word Cloud is a collaborative brainstorming tool. The facilitator chooses a theme — for example, 'What word describes our team culture?' or 'What is one goal for this quarter?' — and each team member contributes a word. The words are displayed together as a visual cloud, with repeated words growing larger. The result is an instant snapshot of group sentiment that can guide discussion, reveal alignment (or misalignment), and create a shared artifact for the team.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Choose a theme or question for the word cloud. Use the tool for inspiration.
  2. Share the prompt with the group. Give everyone 30 seconds to think.
  3. Each person contributes one word (or short phrase) via the tool or by typing in chat.
  4. The facilitator reads the collected words aloud, noting which words appeared most frequently.
  5. Discuss patterns: what words dominated? What surprised you? What is missing?
  6. Save or screenshot the word cloud as a team artifact for future reference.

Tips for Hosts

  • Choose an open-ended prompt that invites diverse responses.
  • If a word appears many times, pause to acknowledge that shared sentiment.
  • For large groups, collect words via chat and enter them into the tool manually.
  • Use word clouds at the start and end of a workshop to measure shifts in sentiment.
  • Do not over-interpret. A word cloud is a conversation starter, not a scientific instrument.

Best Use Cases

  • Team retrospectives: Capture how the team felt about a project or sprint.
  • Values workshops: Identify the values that matter most to the group.
  • Meeting kickoffs: A creative way to check the team's focus or energy.
  • Brainstorming sessions: Warm up creative thinking before diving into problem-solving.
