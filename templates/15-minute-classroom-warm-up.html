⏱️ 15-Minute Classroom Warm-Up

A quick, low-pressure 15-minute warm-up for starting class, re-engaging after a break, or opening a discussion. Every activity is optional — students can pass at any time.

When to Use This Warm-Up

Use this 15-minute warm-up at the start of class, after lunch or a long break, before beginning a group discussion, or when student energy feels low. The short format fits into any lesson plan without taking too much time.

15-Minute Plan

TimeActivity
0–2 minWelcome and pass option
2–5 minOne Word Check-In
5–9 minThis or That
9–13 minQuestion Wheel or Guess the Word
13–15 minClosing transition

Teacher Script

"Before we jump into today topic, let us take two minutes to warm up. Everything is optional — you can pass, write instead of speak, or just listen. What is one word for how you are feeling today? I will go first..."

Versions

Small Group (3-8): Go around and let each student share individually.

Large Class (20+): Use hand-raising for This or That. For Question Wheel, ask for 3-4 volunteers.

Online Class: Share screen for game tools. Use chat for responses. Cameras optional.

FAQ

When should I use this warm-up?

At the start of class, after a break, or when energy is low.

Can this work for large classes?

Yes. Use chat, hand-raising, or small groups.

Can I use this online?

Yes. Online version included with chat and screen sharing.

What if a student passes?

Let them pass. Participation is always optional.
